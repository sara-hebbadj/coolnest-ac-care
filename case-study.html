How Sliubar Designed a Complete Digital Service Experience for CoolNest

A portfolio case study exploring how a Dubai AC maintenance business could move from scattered enquiries to structured booking, customer guidance, communication workflows, and a central owner dashboard.

Project overview

A fictional business, built like a real service experience.

CoolNest AC Care is a fictional residential AC company created as a Sliubar concept project. It serves as a realistic demonstration of how digital tools could simplify service discovery, appointment requests, and internal organisation.

No real customer, testimonial, certification, integration, or business performance claim is presented. The project is intended for design practice, front-end development, and portfolio demonstration.

The challenge

Too much customer information was trapped in conversations.

The fictional owner relied on calls, Instagram messages, and WhatsApp to understand each job and arrange every appointment manually.

💬

Scattered enquiries

Important details could sit across multiple channels without a consistent format.

❓

Incomplete requests

Customers often asked for a price without explaining the issue, property type, or number of units.

📞

Repeated questions

The owner had to answer the same service, timing, and coverage questions manually.

📆

Manual scheduling

Availability checks and appointment coordination depended on back-and-forth messages.

🔕

Missed follow-ups

Without reminders or a central history, maintenance renewals and leads could be forgotten.

📉

No clear overview

The owner lacked a simple view of requests, statuses, service demand, or estimated value.

The Sliubar solution

One connected journey for customers and the owner.

Instead of treating the website, booking form, assistant, reminders, and dashboard as separate products, the concept connects them into one service experience.

  • Conversion-focused website with clear services, trust cues, and direct booking paths.
  • Service recommendation experience that turns common symptoms into a sensible starting point.
  • Smart multi-step booking that collects the information the owner actually needs.
  • Simulated AI customer assistant using predefined browser logic and honest safety guidance.
  • Customer communication workflows showing how confirmations and reminders could be organised.
  • Owner dashboard for bookings, statuses, filters, analytics, and maintenance follow-up concepts.

    Project scope

    Everything built for the prototype.

    Premium responsive website

    Homepage, service information, FAQs, service areas, and calls to action.

    Service diagnosis experience

    Issue selection with JavaScript recommendations and booking handoff.

    Detailed service catalogue

    Symptoms, inclusions, suitability, comparison, and demo pricing.

    Five-step booking flow

    Validation, appointment choices, review, and local record creation.

    Confirmation experience

    Reference, summary, status, and simulated next-step timeline.

    Nora assistant

    Keyword recognition, structured questions, safety message, and booking link.

    Owner dashboard

    Login concept, stats, table, search, filters, sorting, details, and status controls.

    Local data layer

    Browser localStorage for bookings and demonstration data.

    Automation concepts

    After-booking, pre-appointment, and post-service communication flows.

    Honest results

    This prototype demonstrates intent, not proven business performance.

    Because CoolNest is fictional, the case study does not report conversion lifts, revenue increases, customer satisfaction scores, or operational savings.

    Prototype objective

    Demonstrate a complete customer and owner experience using only HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and localStorage.

    Intended business impact

    Designed to reduce incomplete booking enquiries and make the path to an appointment easier to understand.

    Expected operational benefit

    Designed to centralise customer, property, appointment, and status information.

    Communication goal

    Intended to improve follow-up consistency through clear confirmation and reminder concepts.

    Automation design

    Communication around the service, not just the sale.

    The project maps the messages a customer and owner could need before and after an appointment. These are simulated workflow designs, not live integrations.
